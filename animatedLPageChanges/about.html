Dmitry Yarochkin - master instrumentalist, philosopher, artist. Secondary education received at the lyceum at the St. Petersburg Academy of Arts and Industry named after AL Stiglitz In 2010 he received a bachelor's degree from the Institute of Philosophy of St. Petersburg State University In 2013 he received a master's degree at the Department of Ethics at the Institute of Philosophy of St. Petersburg State University In 2019 he defended his dissertation on philosophical anthropology and making musical instruments He studied the philosophy of Buddhism for more than 10 years. He is the author of a number of articles and monographs in scientific journals. Since 2010 he has been making various musical instruments, specializing mainly in tambourines. Since 2014, Yarochkin's musical instruments have been exhibited in various exhibitions and expositions (in particular in the Gallery of Experimental Sound). Yarochkin's musical instruments are used at Lenfilm, ethnic festivals, photo shoots of various art photographers, etc. In 2019, Yarochkin's musical instruments were exhibited in the House of Composers (St. Petersburg, 45 Bolshaya Morskaya Street) and in the National Center Gallery (St. Petersburg, 166 Nevsky Prospekt). "Musical instruments are perceived by the master as symbols of culture forming a person. Instrumentalism here is anthropotechnics. Yarochkin's instruments are sounding, we can say sonorous sculptures" Anatoly Petrov , editor-in-chief of the encyclopedic site about contemporary artists
